## ➯ Accessibility and Performance - Marcy Stutton Todd
<ul> 
    <li>How quickly a website loads, becomes interactive, and responds to a user’s actions</li>    
    <li>Create digital spaces without barriers for accessibility</li>
    <li><strong>Test Examples:</strong> <br>
        └ Skeleton loaders <br>
        └ device access in any locale <br>        
        └ Test & reflow <br>
        └ Keystroke-level performance (measured by number of keystrokes/commands)
    </li>
    <li>Make sure you have tab focus on the specific part without accidentally tabbing out or behind it</li>
    <li>Make sure your link has enough information without visual cues ("back to ..." instead of just "back")</li>
    <li>Number of seconds before a screen reader can read the page aloud</li>
    <li><strong>How to minimize those seconds?</strong> <br>
        └ Minimize JS, <br> 
        └ Use browser-default HTML + CSS
    </li>
    <li><strong>Reduced motion support!!!</strong> <br>
        └ @media (prefers-reduced-motion)
    </li>
    <li><strong>Why?</strong> <br>
        └ Disabled people need access  <br>
        └ EU law  <br> 
        └ Build better software  <br> 
        └ It's cool
    </li>
    <li>The earlier you start, the better (and cheaper)</li>
    <li>"Go forth and improve access"</li>
    <li>"Leave it better than you found it"</li>
</ul>
